RESCUE
VOSD is the proud orchestrator of the world's largest citywide rescue for dogs. Any dog in distress can be reported via the Rescue or Donate tab on our official Facebook page. You will receive updates about the condition of every dog you report for rescue, along with photographs and descriptions via VOSD Live Updates to your email ID, as well as SMS updates to your registered mobile number.

From rescue to rehabilitation, each and every dog is offered 360 care. Regardless of breed, size, age, or injury, every reported dog is taken only to the city's most well-known private veterinarians to ensure quality treatment, no matter the cost.

We are sucessfully rescuing and treating over 5000 dogs since 2012 - even those with chronic conditions - whom we were told had "no chance of survival". We take no shortcuts in saving lives.

RESCUE TAILS
With approximately 20 injured or unwell dogs reported every single day, the VOSD team has actively rescued thousands of dogs in a short span of just 3-and-a-half years. While more detailed information is chronicled on our Facebook handle, this gallery is just the tip of the iceberg, to help you get a quick glance at VOSD's level of care in every single scenario :

Angad

Date Rescued : Feb 15th, 2013
His Story : An old abandoned lab, Angad was found wandering the streets with a shoulder wound full of maggots.

Sundari

Date Rescued : May 18th, 2013
Her Story : Darling Sundari, meaning 'beautiful one', needed a brace as her femur was completely severed in a hit-and-run.


Husna

Date Rescued : April 2nd, 2013
Her Story : This little angel lost half her snout thanks to a heartless biker. She now lives happily at the VOSD Sanctuary.

Barfi

Date Rescued : Jan 15th, 2014
His Story : Born with a congenital vertebral defect, Barfi's hind legs were completely limp. He's now beter thanks to continual infrared treatment.


Cinnamon

Date Rescued : Dec 13th, 2014
Her Story : With a neck that was partially severed and a maggot wound, Cinammon almost had no chance of survival - until she came to us.

Rocket

Date Rescued : Aug 16th, 2015
Her Story : Suffering from severe skin disease, Rocket came to us almost hairless. Now, even we can't imagine him without his fur!
